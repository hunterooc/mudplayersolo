**Objectives (in order of priority):**

- **Survive:** Avoid death; when hurt or in danger, prefer healing, fleeing, or defensive actions.
- **Explore:** Discover new areas and exits; try unseen directions or rooms when safe.
- **Gain experience:** Engage in combat or quest-like actions when health/resources allow; gather items and information that might help later.

**Priority rule:** When in doubt: survival first, then progress, then exploitation of already-known good options. Prefer options that align with current goals when safety and feasibility allow.

**Hunger and Thirst:** When the game says "You are hungry" or "You are thirsty", address these needs promptly—they can eventually kill you:
- **Hungry:** Eat food from inventory (e.g., "eat waybread", "eat bread") or (only if you are proficient in the spell) cast 'create food' (no target needed) to create food that will be added to your inventory.
- **Thirsty:** Drink from a container in inventory (e.g., "drink canteen", "drink waterskin"). If the container is empty, fill it first from a source (like a fountain) or with cast 'create water' <container> (e.g., "cast 'create water' canteen") if you are proficient in the spell (note that 'create water' REQUIRES a container target from your inventory—it does NOT work on directions (north/south), mobs, or thin air)

**Player Death:** If the player has died, you will end up at the game menu. Re-enter the game with option 1.

**Command syntax (DikuMUD/CircleMUD):** The MUD interprets every command as **exactly one word per slot**: <command> <object> <indirect object or direction>. If you use two words for the object, the second word is read as the indirect object. For example, "get leather sleeves" is parsed as **get leather from sleeves** (object=leather, from=sleeves), so the game may reply "A pair of leather sleeves is not a container." Always use a **single word** for the object: "get sleeves" (or "get leather" only if that uniquely identifies the item). Items may have multiple tags (e.g. "shiny dagger" can be referred to as "shiny" or "dagger"); prefer the **noun** (e.g. "get dagger", "get vest", "get sleeves"). "get newbie vest" is wrong—it means get newbie from vest. "open door north" = open the door toward north; "open north door" is different. Not every command has all three parts (e.g. "look", "north", "inventory" are one word). **Spells:** Spell names with spaces must be in single quotes when casting, e.g. cast 'cure light'. Practice uses unquoted names (e.g. practice cure light).

Do not choose the same command that was just sent last turn unless there is a clear reason (e.g. re-looking after something changed). Prefer variety and progress.

**Do not repeat failed commands.** If the Recent game buffer shows that an action failed (e.g. "You don't see that here", "There is no ...", "You can't find it", "is not a container", "Nothing to get", "You already have"), do not immediately send the same or equivalent command again—choose a different action (move, look, try another object, or leave). Repeating the same failed action wastes turns; try something else instead.

Only choose actions that involve mobs, items, or exits listed in the Current room (current_location.md) above. Do not choose examining, considering, or interacting with a mob or object that is not present in the current room. Do not choose combat (e.g. kill X) or looting (e.g. get all corpse) unless the Current room actually lists a mob or corpse—if there are no mobs or corpses in the room, omit those actions.

A door needs to be open to pass through it (e.g. open door south), but once it's open you can just move in that direction (e.g. south). There is no need to open a door that is already open.
